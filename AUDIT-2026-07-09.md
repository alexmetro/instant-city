## 1. Controls

**C1 — P0 — Pan dead-zone at minimum altitude ("can't navigate left/right when zoomed in," reproduced).**
- Symptom: at min zoom (rig distance clamped to `CFG.minAltitude=30`, which resolves to an actual on-screen altitude of ~7–9 m because `elevationForDistance()` also drops the camera pitch to ~14° at min distance — a grazing, near-horizontal view), dragging the mouse in the upper portion of the screen produces **zero** camera movement. Reproduced with dispatched `pointerdown/move/up` events: an identical horizontal drag (mid-screen height) moved the camera substantially; the same drag at 15% down from the top of the viewport produced a pixel-identical before/after screenshot.
- Root cause (`index.html` ~4657–4679, `screenPointToGround()` / `applyPanPixels()`): panning works by ray-casting the screen point to the horizontal ground plane through `rig.target.y`. At a shallow ~14° camera pitch, the horizon sits much higher in the frame than at higher altitudes, so a large fraction of the screen's upper portion has no ground intersection (`t<0`) or an extremely distant/unstable one. `applyPanPixels()` silently no-ops when either ray misses ("Falls back to a no-op... rather than flinging the camera off an undefined intersection" — comment at line ~4671) — safe, but the practical effect is that panning appears to stop responding whenever the cursor drifts into the now-much-larger sky/horizon region, which is exactly where a user's mouse naturally sits during a horizontal drag.
- Fix: when the ground ray misses, fall back to a yaw-style rotation proportional to horizontal screen delta (so the drag still *does something* rather than nothing), or clamp the intersection to a large-but-finite forward distance instead of rejecting it outright, or raise the minimum elevation angle so the horizon can never occupy so much of the frame at min zoom.

**C2 — P1 — Scroll-zoom has no per-event clamp; a single wheel burst can jump the view by orders of magnitude.**
- `factor = Math.exp(e.deltaY*0.0016)` is applied directly to `desired.distance` with no ceiling on `deltaY` itself. Verified: 20 synthetic `wheel` events at `deltaY:-300` took the rig from 8828 m to 37 m (~240× zoom) in under a second; real trackpads/high-resolution mice commonly deliver `deltaY` bursts of several hundred to 1000+ in one event, which would produce an equally large jump. This is a strong candidate for the "zoom feels too fast" complaint.
- Fix: clamp the per-event factor, e.g. `factor = clamp(Math.exp(e.deltaY*0.0016), 0.85, 1.18)`, so no single wheel tick can move more than ~15–18%.

**C3 — P1 — Zoom is not cursor-anchored (focal-point drift).**
- The `wheel` handler only mutates `desired.distance`; it never adjusts `rig.target` toward the point under the cursor. Because `elevationForDistance()` couples camera *pitch* to *distance*, the ground point that stays fixed while scrolling is always whatever `rig.target` currently is — never the point under the mouse. The exact fix already exists in this file for touch: `pinchGestureMove()` computes an `anchorWorld` and re-projects it back under the pinch midpoint every frame (~line 4747–4763). The mouse wheel path has no equivalent, so scrolling on desktop makes the ground visibly slide out from under the cursor.
- Fix: port the pinch anchor-correction pattern to the wheel handler (raycast the point under the cursor before changing distance, then nudge `rig.target` so it re-projects to the same screen point after).

**C4 — P1 — Touch pan / pinch-zoom / twist-rotate: verified functionally correct.**
- Dispatched synthetic `touchstart/move/end` (1-finger pan) and 2-finger pinch-out + twist sequences on the canvas at 375×812: both produced correct, proportionate camera response (pan moved the ground under the finger; pinch zoomed with the correct sign). No functional regression found in the touch path itself — note C1's dead-zone bug applies equally here since it shares `screenPointToGround`.

**C5 — P2 — WASD/Q-E/R-F: no functional bugs.** Q/E orbit the camera *around* `rig.target` rather than turning the view direction in place (FPS-style head-turn). Reasonable for a map camera; flag only because it will feel unintuitive if walk-mode (grounding.md) is later built on top of this same rig without adding a true look-around control.

## 2. Scale Sweep

Method: altitude read from the live HUD (ground-truth, not estimated); dimensions read directly from the geometry-construction code (`makeFullShipHullGeo`, `makeBrigHullGeo`, `makeSchoonerHullGeo`, `makePersonGeo`, `placeBuilding`, `buildStreets`, `makeTentGeo`, `buildWharf`), cross-checked visually against screenshots at 100 m / 300 m / 1000 m.

**S1 — P1 — Ships are undersized 40–60% vs. documented real dimensions.**
| Type | Sim hull length | Real-world comparable (geography-shoreline.md) | Deviation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full ship (3-mast) | 17 m | General Harrison 126 ft = 38.4 m; typical full-rigged merchantman 100–150 ft (30–46 m) | **−55%** vs. General Harrison |
| Brig | 13.5 m | Documented brigs of the period ~24–30 m | **−48%** |
| Schooner | 11.5 m | Documented schooners ~18–27 m | **−46%** |

All exceed the 30% "flag it" threshold. This is the same magnitude of scale dishonesty as inflating — QA-GATE's rule ("never inflate geometry to make something visible") should be read as "never distort scale in either direction," and undersizing ships this much is a direct violation. Recommend rebuilding hull length constants to true scale (~35 m / ~26 m / ~20 m) and re-checking anchor-slot spacing/instance caps still fit the intended draw-call budget at the corrected size.

**S2 — P1 — Person figures are short of the documented eye-height target.** `makePersonGeo()`: coat spans y=0 to 1.15, head sphere top at y=1.28+0.15=**1.43 m**. Grounding.md's walk-mode section targets a 1.7 m eye height for an adult (implying ~1.65–1.75 m standing height). Current figures are ~15–20% short — closer to a child's height than an adult's. Fix: scale up ~18% (coat height, head/hat offsets) to land near 1.7 m.

**S3 — P0 — True walk-mode (1.7 m eye height) is not actually reachable.** The camera-rig terrain collision floor is `minY = groundAtCam + 4.5` (index.html ~4605) — **4.5 m**, not 1.7 m. Combined with the ~14° pitch at min zoom (grazing aerial view, not a standing-eye view) and the C1 pan dead-zone, the lowest available camera position is neither at walk-mode height nor reliably navigable. If walk-mode is intended to already exist (grounding.md frames it as "committed," with a note that "every asset must survive bubble-distance viewing"), this is a P0 gap between documentation and reality; if it's explicitly future work, the docs should say so more clearly at the point CFG.minAltitude is defined.

**S4 — P2 — Streets are 10 m (main) / 7 m (cross), not "12 m."** Not a bug — the code's own comment explains the 12 m figure this audit was briefed with isn't in the sourced record (only Market St.'s exceptional 120 ft is), and 10/7 m dirt-track widths are a reasonable period read. Documented here only so future audits calibrate against the actual constant, not the brief's approximation.

**S5 — Buildings (4.5–8 m × 3.8–6.8 m × 2.6–4.2 m) and tents (~3.4 m diameter × 2.6 m tall): no scale issues found.** Both read as plausible true-scale single-story cottages / miner's canvas tents.

## 3. Historical Presence Check

**H1 — P0 — Wharf growth over time is completely unmodeled.** `buildWharf()` (index.html ~3080–3140) constructs a single static mesh once at load: one 16 m deck + one 13 m deck + 2 rowboats + 1 scow, with **zero** date-dependency anywhere in the function. `research/geography-shoreline.md` §4 documents Central/Long Wharf reaching **800 ft (≈240 m)** by 1849 and **~6,000 ft aggregate wharfage across 12 wharves** by fall 1850, plus named wharves at Clark's Point/Broadway, Clay St, Pacific St, Market St, and Howison's Pier — none of this exists or grows in the sim; the wharf looks the same in July 1846 and October 1849.
- Fix: parametrize at minimum the Central Wharf deck length by `simDay` (short stub pre-1848 → ~240 m by late 1849), and add the other named wharves as they historically opened.

**H2 — P0 — No ship-abandonment / storeship-conversion visual, and the three most iconic storeships are absent from the data entirely.** `updateShips()` only distinguishes "sailing" (sails set) vs. "anchored" (sails furled, scale 0.02, gentle bob) — there is no additional state for a vessel that has sat derelict for months, which is exactly what happened to hundreds of ships in real 1849 SF as crews deserted for the goldfields. A ship with `depart:null` (no recorded departure) looks pixel-identical the day it arrives and six months later. Searched both `events-1846-49.js` and `index.html` for `Niantic|Apollo|Euphemia|storeship`: **zero matches** — none of the three best-documented storeships (Niantic arrived Apr 1849, Apollo Sept 1849, Euphemia Apr 1849, all well within this build's covered window) appear as named ships anywhere.
- Fix: add a "long-anchored"/derelict visual variant (list angle, patched/torn furled sail, gull-perch dots) past some anchored-day threshold; add Niantic/Apollo/Euphemia/General Harrison as explicit `ship_arrival` events with a distinguishing storeship hull treatment (deck shed, "HOTEL"/"STOREHOUSE" signage) once converted.

**H3 — P0 — Happy Valley tent camps do not render at the documented date/location.** Flew to the coded cluster center `(900, 950)` (matches `DISTRICT_ZONES`, `tentCandidates`, and the pathfinding graph consistently) on **1849-06-01**, when `populationAt()` implies pop ≈ 11,000 → `growthTargets()` implies ≈1,257 tents should already be revealed. At altitudes 120 m, 271 m, and 700 m: bare dune/scrub terrain, a handful of procedural bushes, **no tents, no campfires, no people, no paths** anywhere in the ~650 m sample radius (screenshots captured). The QA-GATE's own known-debt note attributes this to a contrast/silhouette problem ("tents blend into sand") — but the tent color was already changed to a cool off-white specifically to fix that (code comment at `makeTentGeo()`), and still nothing is visible, suggesting the deeper problem is that **few or zero tent candidates are actually being generated/accepted** in this cluster (possibly because the coded cluster center sits east of the analytic shoreline model — see `shorelineX(950)≈410` vs. cluster `cx:900` — so the rejection-sampling radius may mostly land on water per the real baked heightmap). Same emptiness found at the western hillside camp cluster `(-1088,-268)`.
- Fix: instrument `tentCandidates` at build time to log per-cluster accepted-sample counts; if Happy Valley's count is near zero, re-derive the cluster center against the baked terrain (not the simplified analytic `shorelineX()`), and re-verify the growth-reveal system is actually running (it should self-catch-up instantly on a hash-jump per `growReveal()`'s `while` loop, so this isn't a pacing issue).

**H4 — P0 — Mission Dolores and the Presidio "outpost" clusters render as empty fields.** Flew to the exact documented coordinates (`OUTPOSTS.mission = (-1997, 3462)`, `OUTPOSTS.presidio = (-4776, -234)`, both cited in-code to `research/peninsula-1846.md`) at 1847-06-01, altitude 200–400 m (covering the full ±110 m jitter radius): Mission Dolores shows only ambient tree/bush scatter, **zero** of the intended 7 adobe buildings; the Presidio shows **nothing at all** — not even scatter — despite being the site of the July 9, 1846 flag-raising the game's own HUD event ticker cites. Likely cause: `buildOutposts()`'s per-attempt gate `if(y<1) continue;` (index.html ~1422) silently drops any jittered point whose baked terrain height is below 1 m — plausible if these two real-world-sourced coordinates sit on locally low/marshy ground in the baked heightmap, in which case most or all of the 7/6 attempts could be failing.
- Fix: log the pass/fail count for both clusters at build time; if it's 0 or near-0, widen the accepted height band, sample the cluster center's own height directly rather than only the jittered points, or nudge failing points toward the nearest valid ground instead of discarding them.

**H5 — Weather/season/rain-year system: no issues found.** Correctly cites `weather-climate.md`'s 1849–50 "mud winter" (33.10" instrumental record) vs. earlier undocumented years defaulting to a stated placeholder intensity — a good example of the "silences named as silences" design law being followed correctly.

## 4. Visual Artifacts

**V1 — P1 — Sawtooth/zigzag shoreline mesh at the Mission Creek / Islais Creek inlet.** At the southern end of the map (near the Happy Valley cluster, world z≈950, visible at 271–540 m altitude), the water/land boundary of a creek-mouth inlet renders as a harsh, regular triangular zigzag rather than a smooth or naturalistically irregular edge — clearly a low terrain-mesh resolution artifact showing through, not an intentional rocky-shore texture (the repeat interval is too uniform). Screenshot evidence captured at world position ~(500,950), altitude 271 m.
- Fix: increase local mesh resolution around creek mouths/inlets, or apply a shoreline-smoothing/organic-noise pass distinct from the open-bay shoreline treatment.

**V2 — P2 — Console warning spam.** `THREE.BufferGeometry.toNonIndexed()` fires "already non-indexed" warnings 40+ times on every single page load (from ship/person/tent prefab construction). Harmless to rendering but clutters devtools for future debugging and represents small redundant work at every load.
- Fix: guard the call with `if (geo.index) geo = geo.toNonIndexed();`.

**V3 — P2 — Full night reads as "very dark," bordering on illegible.** At full night (`nightFactor≈1`: ambient 0.05–0.14, hemi 0.12), terrain/building/district differentiation is nearly lost — appropriate for an 1849 no-electric-light setting, but a first-time viewer may read it as the app having broken/gone black rather than a deliberate night state, especially since it can arrive within minutes of idle viewing (`DAY_CYCLE_SECONDS=420`, i.e. a 7-minute full day/night loop). HUD panels themselves stay legible (cream text on dark parchment retains contrast).
- Fix (optional): a subtle moonlight rim/fill light floor so terrain silhouette remains readable at deep night without being anachronistic.

**V4 — Not reproduced, flagging only as a spot-check note.** During one heavy round of rapid automated reloads, the "ASSEMBLING THE PENINSULA…" loading screen appeared to hang past 5+ seconds with `#hud-loading` never hidden and the `[verify] terrain-1846.js loaded` log never printing for that specific load. A clean subsequent reload loaded normally in the expected time. Likely an artifact of this audit's own rapid-fire reload cadence rather than real user behavior, but worth a human spot-check under normal usage.

## 7. Placement Collision Sweep

Sampled the village core at low altitude (28–66 m) in both 1847-06-01 and 1849-06-01. Because buildings are placed once at world-build time (`placeBuilding()` is called from a single `buildVillage()` IIFE at load) and only *progressively revealed* per era rather than re-placed, the same static layout — and the same collisions — appear identically in both eras; the bug is era-independent.

**Findings (counted from screenshots at world position ≈(-125,-8) to (-125,-30), a single ~150 m × 150 m window):**
- **≥5 buildings** with a roof/footprint corner or edge sitting directly on the dirt street strip or a street intersection rather than fully inside a lot (clear photographic evidence at 28–66 m altitude — buildings visibly straddle the dirt road texture).
- The user's own observation ("houses spawned ON roads") is confirmed, not isolated to one seed/session.

**Root cause — this is an engine gap, not a per-building bug:**
- `placeBuilding(x,z,rotBase)` (~line 1204) gates only on `terrainHeight(x,z) <= 1` (below-land check) before placing a 4.5–8 m building. It never consults the street geometry built separately in `buildStreets()`/`drapeGroundStrip()` (~line 1087).
- `buildOutposts()` (~line 1412) gates only on the same height check.
- `tentCandidates`/`growthBuildingCandidates` (~lines 1967, 1987) gate on `farFromVillage()` + a height/biome range — again, no street/road awareness, and no awareness of each other or of already-placed buildings.
- **No spawner in this file checks against the street graph, the Mission-road corridor, water depth beyond a single height threshold, slope, or any other spawner's already-accepted footprints.** Each of the four spawner systems (village buildings, growth buildings, tents, outposts) independently rejection-samples against its own narrow criteria only.

**Recommendation:** implement one shared `canPlace(x, z, footprintW, footprintD)` service, consulted by *every* spawner before accepting a candidate position, checking:
1. Distance from the candidate's footprint to the nearest street/road/Mission-trail centerline ≥ (street half-width + margin).
2. Terrain slope ≤ ~20° and height above water (reject underwater/near-shore per existing per-spawner logic, but centralized).
3. Bounding-box overlap against a shared, incrementally-populated spatial index of every already-accepted placement across *all* classes (buildings, tents, outposts, wharf props), not just its own class.

This replaces four independent, inconsistent rejection-sampling implementations with one, and is the only way to guarantee the class of bug (buildings-on-roads, and by extension any future building-on-building or tent-on-building overlap) doesn't keep recurring piecemeal as new asset types are added — which is exactly the pattern QA-GATE's "Current known debts" section already shows happening (tents, ships, and town density were each patched separately after the fact).

## Fix-First Shortlist (top 5)

1. **H3/H4/D1–D4 — Make the four documented settlement sites (Happy Valley, western hillside camps, Mission Dolores, the Presidio) actually render something.** This is the single biggest "instant city" credibility gap — right now the record's best-known secondary sites are empty fields. 5. **C2/C3 — Clamp scroll-zoom sensitivity and anchor it to the cursor** (port the existing pinch-anchor logic to the wheel handler). Cheapest fix on this list, directly addresses the "controls feel funky" complaint's most-reported symptom.

## Top 3 Issues
1. Four documented settlement sites (Happy Valley, hillside camps, Mission Dolores, Presidio) render as empty fields at dates the record says they're inhabited (P0).
2. Buildings placed directly on streets/intersections — no shared collision-aware placement service exists across any spawner (P0/systemic).
3. Camera pan stops responding whenever the cursor is in the screen's upper portion at minimum zoom — the exact, reproduced cause of "can't navigate left/right when zoomed in" (P0).
